# Continuation

## What Problem Were We Facing?

In previous chapters, we learned:

```text
Coroutine
↓
Can Suspend

Coroutine
↓
Can Resume
```

This immediately raises an important question:

```text
How Does Kotlin Know Where To Resume?
```

Consider:

```text
Step 1
↓
Step 2
↓
Suspend
↓
Resume
```

When the coroutine resumes:

```text
How Does It Know To Continue From Step 3?
```

Why doesn't it restart from:

```text
Step 1?
```

To answer this question, we must understand:

```text
Continuation
```

## First Intuition

Imagine reading a book.

You stop reading at:

```text
Page 125
```

and place a bookmark.

Later you return.

How do you know where to continue?

Because of:

```text
Bookmark
```

Without the bookmark:

```text
Find Location Again
```

would be much harder.

A Continuation plays a similar role for a coroutine.

---

## The Core Problem

Suppose we have:

```text
Step 1
↓
Step 2
↓
Suspend
↓
Resume
↓
Step 3
```

When suspension happens, Kotlin must somehow remember:

```text
Where Did We Stop?
```

Otherwise resuming would be impossible.

Conceptually:

```text
Suspend
↓
Remember Position
↓
Resume
↓
Continue Correctly
```

---

## Definition

A Continuation is Kotlin's mechanism for remembering how a suspended coroutine can continue later.

Conceptually:

```text
Suspend
↓
Remember What Comes Next
↓
Resume
↓
Continue Execution
```

The most important idea is:

```text
Continuation
↓
Remembers How To Continue
```

---

## Mental Model

Think of a Continuation as:

```text
A Bookmark For Execution
```

Just as a bookmark remembers:

```text
Where To Continue Reading
```

a Continuation remembers:

```text
Where To Continue Executing
```

---

## What Must Be Remembered?

A common misconception is:

```text
Continuation
↓
Only Stores A Line Number
```

Not quite.

Imagine:

```kotlin
val user = getUser()
showUser(user)
```

Suppose suspension happens while waiting for:

```kotlin
getUser()
```

When execution continues, Kotlin must know:

```text
What To Do Next
```

which in this case is:

```kotlin
showUser(user)
```

Conceptually:

```text
Current Position

What Comes Next

Required State
```

must all be available so execution can continue correctly.

At this stage, it is enough to understand:

```text
Continuation
↓
Stores Enough Information To Continue Later
```

## Another Mental Model

Imagine a movie.

You pause at:

```text
01:25:18
```

Later you press:

```text
Play
```

The movie does not restart.

It continues from the paused position.

Why?

Because the player remembered:

```text
Where Playback Stopped
```

A Continuation serves a similar purpose.

## Suspension And Continuation

Notice how these concepts connect.

```text
Suspend
↓
Pause

Continuation
↓
Remember How To Continue

Resume
↓
Continue Later
```

Suspension without Continuation would be useless.

The coroutine would have no way to continue correctly.

---

## Why Can't Kotlin Suspend Anywhere?

Earlier we deliberately postponed this question.

A natural thought is:

```text
Why Doesn't Kotlin Allow Suspension Everywhere?
```

To support suspension, Kotlin must be able to:

```text
Pause
↓
Remember State
↓
Continue Later
```

Every possible suspension location creates another location that Kotlin may need to remember and restore later.

Because of this, suspension is restricted to:

```text
Suspension Points
```

which are carefully defined locations where Kotlin knows suspension may occur.

This makes coroutine behavior more structured and predictable.

---

## Important Clarification

A Continuation is NOT:

```text
The Coroutine Itself
```

The coroutine represents:

```text
Work Being Performed
```

The Continuation represents:

```text
How To Continue That Work
```

These are related but different concepts.

---

## Another Important Clarification

A Continuation is NOT:

```text
A Thread
```

Earlier we learned:

```text
Thread
↓
Provides Execution
```

A Continuation does not execute anything.

Its responsibility is remembering how execution can continue later.

---

## Why Is This Important?

Without Continuations:

```text
Suspend
↓
Resume
```

would not work.

Kotlin would have no way to know:

```text
Where To Continue

What To Execute Next
```

A Continuation is one of the key ideas that makes coroutine suspension possible.

## Common Misconceptions

### Misconception 1

```text
Continuation = Coroutine
```

Incorrect.

A coroutine is the work.

A continuation remembers how to continue that work.

---

### Misconception 2

```text
Continuation = Thread
```

Incorrect.

A continuation does not provide execution.

---

### Misconception 3

```text
Continuation Only Remembers A Line Number
```

Oversimplified.

It remembers enough information for execution to continue correctly.

---

### Misconception 4

```text
Resume Works Without Continuation
```

Incorrect.

Continuation is what makes resume possible.
